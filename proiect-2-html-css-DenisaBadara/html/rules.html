Facts about rules of Handball

Overview A short history General rules Romanian teams and players
  • Handball teams start the game with 7 players on the court, 1 goalkeeper, and 6 outfield players.
  • The game starts with a throw-off. A player in the middle of the court passes the ball back to a teammate.
  • To score in handball, a player must throw the ball in between the other team's goalposts.
  • Players holding the ball can take 3 steps before they must pass or shoot the ball.
  • Penalty shots are taken from the 7-meter line.
  • During a penalty shot, one of the shooter's feet needs to be planted on the floor the entire time.Jumping is not allowed.
Scoring in Handball

The objective for the players is to throw the ball into the goal. The team that scores the most goals in a match is declared the winner. The game can also end up in a tie. Handball goals often look spectacular. This is because players usually jump over the goal area line, thus ensuring they are not in contact with the goal area, and attempt to throw the ball in the goal while in the air.

How to play Handball

Players can throw, catch, stop, push or hit the ball, by using their hands, head, torso, thighs and knees. They cannot touch the ball with their feet or with any part below the knee.

Handball is a body-contact sport. Furthermore, attacking strategies are encouraged, while passive play leads to a warning and loss of possession for the team.

Handball Fouls

Yellow cards are a warning often awarded to players who have kicked the ball or inadvertently charged into an opponent.

Red cards are given to players for severe fouls such as tripping or unnecessary outbursts of violent physicality. A player or a coach receiving a red card is disqualified from the game.

Players or coaches shown a blue card are disqualified and suspended for multiple subsequent games.Blue cards are only common in professional leagues and the Olympic Games.
